What is Value Added Monthly Index

A value added monthly index (VAMI) tracks the monthly performance of a hypothetical $1000 investment, assuming reinvestment, over a period of time.

Key Takeaway

  • VAMI is one of the most commonly used metrics to depict a fund's overall performance to investors.
  • VAMI is calculated using NET monthly returns.
  • A value added monthly index charts the total return gained by an investor over a specified period of time. It includes capital gains as well as reinvestment of any disbursements, such as dividends and additional interest earned through compounding.
